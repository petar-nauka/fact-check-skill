# Manipulation Red Flags

Use these markers to explain how a claim is framed. Red flags are not proof that a
claim is false; they are reasons to slow down and verify carefully.

## A. Emotional Manipulation

- A1: Fear, panic, outrage, disgust.
- A2: Loaded insults or dehumanizing language.
- A3: Urgent calls to share before deletion.
- A4: Us-versus-them framing.
- A5: Victimhood or persecution narrative.

## B. Source Opacity

- B1: No named author or accountable organization.
- B2: Anonymous insider, unnamed expert, or "sources say" with no detail.
- B3: Imitation domain or misleading page branding.
- B4: No corrections policy, editorial standards, or funding transparency.
- B5: Citation chain loops back to blogs, social posts, or the same source network.

## C. Context Manipulation

- C1: Old event presented as current.
- C2: Foreign event presented as local.
- C3: Quote stripped of qualifiers.
- C4: Real document/image used to imply something unsupported.
- C5: Satire, opinion, or speculation presented as news.

## D. Conspiracy and Delegitimization

- D1: Hidden cabal or "they do not want you to know" framing.
- D2: Blanket dismissal of all mainstream sources without evidence.
- D3: Preemptive claim that any correction proves censorship.
- D4: False dilemma: only two extreme choices presented.
- D5: Guilt by association.

## E. Statistical Abuse

- E1: Percentages without denominators.
- E2: Correlation presented as causation.
- E3: Cherry-picked timeframe.
- E4: Anecdote used against broad evidence.
- E5: Raw adverse-event reports presented as proven causation.
- E6: Graph axis or scale distortion.

## F. Synthetic or Media Manipulation

- F1: Cropped screenshot without linkable source.
- F2: Inconsistent typography, lighting, shadows, or compression artifacts.
- F3: No original upload or provenance.
- F4: AI-generated profile, image, or voice indicators.
- F5: Misleading thumbnail/headline mismatch.

## Severity Guidance

- Low: Framing issue that does not materially change the factual claim.
- Medium: Red flag affects interpretation, source trust, or context.
- High: Red flag is central to the claim's persuasive force.
- Critical: Red flag could cause health, safety, financial, or civic harm.

Use red flags to inform MTS in MFS scoring, but keep factual verdicts evidence-led.
