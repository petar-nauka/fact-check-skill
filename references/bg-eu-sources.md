# Bulgarian and EU Source Pack

Use this reference for Bulgarian, EU policy, health, science, energy, and
geopolitical claims.

## Bulgarian Fact-Checking and Journalism

- Factcheck.bg - Bulgarian fact-checking.
- AFP Proveri - Bulgarian-language AFP verification.
- Detector.bg - media monitoring and disinformation analysis.
- Mediapool.bg - Bulgarian news with editorial standards.
- Dnevnik.bg - Bulgarian news with editorial standards.
- Capital.bg - business, policy, and economy reporting.
- BTA - Bulgarian News Agency.

## EU and Official Sources

- EUR-Lex - EU law and official legal texts.
- European Commission - policy announcements, directives, programmes.
- European Parliament - votes, adopted texts, parliamentary questions.
- Council of the EU - member-state decisions and conclusions.
- Eurostat - EU statistics.
- ECDC - communicable disease evidence and surveillance.
- EMA - medicines and vaccine safety information.
- WHO Europe - regional health guidance.

## Bulgarian Official Sources

- National Statistical Institute (NSI) - official Bulgarian statistics.
- Ministry of Health - health orders and public-health statements.
- Ministry of Energy - energy policy statements and documents.
- Council of Ministers - government decisions and press releases.
- Central Election Commission - election data and official decisions.
- National Assembly - legislation and parliamentary records.

## Science and Health

- PubMed - biomedical literature search.
- Cochrane Library - systematic reviews.
- Retraction Watch - retractions and research integrity context.
- Google Scholar - broad scholarly discovery; verify papers directly.
- ClinicalTrials.gov and EU Clinical Trials Register - clinical trial status.

## Geopolitical and Disinformation Monitoring

- EUvsDisinfo - documented pro-Kremlin disinformation narratives.
- NATO official site - alliance decisions and statements.
- OSCE - election observation and security context.
- UN, IAEA, IPCC, OECD, World Bank - domain-specific institutional evidence.

## Search Query Templates

Use the original language plus English. Add Russian for claims likely translated
from pro-Kremlin ecosystems.

- Exact claim: `"<core phrase>"`
- Bulgarian fact-check: `<claim terms> factcheck.bg OR proveri.afp.com`
- English fact-check: `<claim terms> fact check Reuters AFP AP`
- EU source: `<claim terms> site:europa.eu OR site:eur-lex.europa.eu`
- Statistics: `<indicator> Eurostat NSI methodology`
- Health: `<condition/treatment> WHO EMA ECDC Cochrane PubMed`
- Russian origin check: `<claim terms in Russian> EUvsDisinfo RT Sputnik`

## Bulgarian Context Notes

- Many local narratives are translated or adapted from Russian and English
  conspiracy ecosystems. Trace origin when wording looks translated.
- Watch for EU/NATO skepticism, energy-policy misinformation, health scares,
  election legitimacy claims, historical revisionism, and minority scapegoating.
- Avoid static blocklists. Evaluate each source and claim on evidence.
