# Reference: Bulgarian Information-Space Context

Load this for any claim touching the Bulgarian information space. Evaluate each claim on its
merits — do **not** maintain a blocklist.

> This file covers **narratives, patterns, and local data points**. For the concrete
> **source lists and search-query templates** (fact-checkers, EU/official sources, science,
> tools), use `bg-eu-sources.md`. The two are complementary.

## Common disinformation patterns

- **Pro-Kremlin narratives** — translated/adapted from RT, Sputnik, Tsargrad and spread via local
  outlets and social media. Themes: NATO as threat, EU as oppressor, Ukraine as puppet, Western
  values as decadent. Exploit real sovereignty concerns toward geopolitical goals.
- **Health misinformation** — anti-vaccine content, miracle cures, alternative medicine
  (homeopathy, Schuessler salts, folk healing). Bulgaria had one of the EU's lowest COVID
  vaccination rates; high institutional distrust and lower health literacy amplify this.
- **Historical revisionism** — Bulgarian-Macedonian / Bulgarian-Turkish framings to inflame
  nationalist sentiment; hard for the public to verify, strong identity-protective pull.
- **EU/NATO skepticism** — reframing legitimate democratic debates (e.g. EU regulation) as tyranny.
- **Energy disinformation** — Belene nuclear, Russian gas dependency, renewables; often promotes
  Russian energy dependency while discrediting the European energy transition.
- **Election-related manipulation** — peaks before national votes: fake polls, fabricated candidate
  statements, voter-suppression narratives, delegitimizing the electoral process.

## Key data points

- 46% of young Bulgarians cannot distinguish fact from opinion online (AEJ-Bulgaria, 2023).
- Low-credibility content spread ~19.2% more than high-credibility content on social media for
  COVID topics (SpringerOpen study).
- Fact-check labels reduced sharing of false news by up to 50% (Meta data, Eastern Europe).
- ~1/3 of people who read a fact-check article about vaccines shifted toward greater trust in
  science (AEJ-Bulgaria).

## Bulgarian fact-checking resources

- **Factcheck.bg** (AEJ-Bulgaria) — IFCN-affiliated
- **AFP Proveri** — AFP Fact Check in Bulgarian
- **Detector.bg** — independent media monitoring and fact-checking
- **Mediapool.bg** — includes a fact-checking section
- **Google Fact Check Explorer** — searchable global fact-check database

## International resources (English)

Snopes, PolitiFact, Full Fact (UK), FactCheck.org / SciCheck, EUvsDisinfo, Bellingcat, BBC Verify,
Reuters Fact Check, Climate Feedback, Health Feedback.

## Verification tools

Google Fact Check Explorer, Google Reverse Image Search, TinEye, Yandex Images (useful for
Russian-language content), InVID/WeVerify, FotoForensics, Deepware Scanner, Wayback Machine
(archive.org), PubMed, Cochrane Library, Google Scholar, EUR-Lex (verify EU-legislation claims).
